Writing Remainders as Decimals

When a division does not come out evenly, we do not have to stop at a remainder. We can keep going into the decimal places and find an exact answer.

Big idea · division with a remainder

Learn the method

A remainder is really a division that has not finished yet. Instead of stopping and writing a remainder, we keep dividing into the decimal places until the division ends. Here is the method, step by step. Replay the walkthrough below to see it happen.

  1. Divide as usual. Set up the bus stop and divide left to right, carrying any leftover to the next digit.
  2. Do not stop at a remainder. When there is a leftover at the end, add a decimal point in your answer, right above the point in the number.
  3. Add a zero and keep dividing. 431 is the same as 431.00, so bring the leftover onto a zero and divide again after the point.
  4. Stop when the leftover is zero. Then the answer is exact. If the digits repeat forever, like 10 ÷ 3 = 3.3333..., round the recurring decimal.
The one rule to remember. Line up the decimal point in your answer directly above the decimal point in the number you are dividing. Everything else is just ordinary division, carried on a little longer.
